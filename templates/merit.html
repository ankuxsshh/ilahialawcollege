About Course

This program leads to a Bachelor’s Degree in Law (LL.B.), structured in accordance with the Bar Council of India (BCI) regulations, as empowered by the Advocates Act, 1961. Upon successful completion, the degree qualifies graduates for enrollment as an Advocate under the Advocates Act, 1961. The course requires a minimum of three academic years of regular study. Additionally, the final six months of the last academic year include mandatory practical training, ensuring students gain hands-on legal experience.

Eligibility & Admission Criteria


  • (i) Nativity Requirements:
    • The applicant must be an Indian citizen.
    • Only candidates of Kerala origin are eligible for reservation benefits and fee concessions.

    Documents to prove Indian citizenship:

    • Birth certificate showing place of birth in India.
    • SSLC showing place of birth.
    • Valid Indian passport.
    • Certificate from a Village Officer certifying nativity.

    Documents to prove Kerala origin:

    • SSLC/birth certificate indicating Kerala as the place of birth.
    • Parent’s passport/SSLC with Kerala as place of birth and supporting relationship certificate.
    • Village Officer/Tahsildar certificate confirming Kerala birth of candidate or parent.
  • (ii) Academic Qualifications:
    • Bachelor’s degree from a recognized university.
    • Minimum 45% for General, 42% for SEBC, and 40% for SC/ST.
    • No rounding of percentages allowed.
    • Distance graduates eligible, open university graduates without prior basic qualifications are not.
    • Final year students may apply but must submit final certificates during admission.
  • (iii) Age Criteria:
    • No lower age limit.
    • Upper age limit subject to Supreme Court decision and BCI rules.

Integrated 5-Year LL.B. – Admission to Merit Seats

The admission procedure for merit seats in the Integrated Five-Year LLB Course in Kerala is conducted by the Commissioner for Entrance Examinations (CEE), Kerala. Here's a detailed overview:

1. Affirming Eligibility

  • Must have passed the Higher Secondary Examination (10+2) or equivalent.
  • Minimum marks:
    • General: 45%
    • SEBC: 42%
    • SC/ST: 40%
  • No upper age limit.

2. Entrance Examination

  • Appear for the CEE Kerala LLB 5-Year Entrance Exam.
  • Exam includes MCQs on:
    • General English
    • General Knowledge
    • Aptitude for Legal Studies
  • Rank list prepared based on exam performance.

3. Online Application & Registration

  • Register on the CEE Kerala website.
  • Pay the application fee online.

4. Rank List & Merit-Based Allotment

  • Rank list published after exam.
  • Seats allotted based on:
    • Entrance rank
    • Reservation norms
    • College preference via option entry

5. Seat Allotment Process

  • CAP rounds conducted by CEE.
  • Confirm seat and pay fees on time.

6. Document Verification & Admission

  • Report to allotted college with documents.
  • Failure to report leads to cancellation.

Fee Structure (Merit Seat)

  • Annual Tuition Fee: ₹35,000
  • Special Fee: ₹5,000
  • Caution Deposit: ₹5,000
  • Refundable Deposit: ₹50,000
