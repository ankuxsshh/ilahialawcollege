Library Committee :

The Library Committee is a body that oversees the functioning and development of the library.

Objectives
  • • Resource Development: Recommend book purchases and resource development.
  • • Library Services: Oversee library services, including circulation, reference, and research support.
  • • User Needs: Identify and address user needs and concerns.
Functions
  • • Collection Development: Recommend books, journals, and digital resources.
  • • Library Policies: Develop and review library policies.
  • • User Feedback: Collect and consider user feedback.
Importance
  • • Resource Availability: Ensures availability of relevant resources for students and faculty.
  • • Library Services: Enhances library services to support learning and research.
  • • User Satisfaction: Promotes user satisfaction with library resources and services.

The Library Committee plays a vital role in supporting the academic mission of the institution by ensuring the library meets the needs of its users.

Legal Aid Clinic :

Provides legal assistance to marginalized communities, promoting access to justice and practical experience for students.

Objectives
  • • Provide Legal Assistance : Offer free or low-cost legal services to marginalized and underprivileged individuals.
  • • Promote Access to Justice : Ensure equal access to justice for all, regardless of economic or social status.
  • • Empower Communities : Educate and empower communities about their legal rights and entitlements.
  • • Foster Social Responsibility : Encourage law students and professionals to contribute to social welfare.
  • • Practical Training : Provide hands-on experience and training for law students in legal practice.

Internal Complaints Committee (ICC):

Handles complaints of sexual harassment, ensures a safe campus environment, and implements preventive measures. The Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) is a statutory body established under the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013. Its purpose is to:

  • • Receive and investigate complaints of sexual harassment
  • • Provide a safe and confidential platform for victims to report incidents
  • • Conduct inquiries and recommend actions to the institution
  • • Implement preventive measures to promote a safe work/study environment

The ICC plays a crucial role in ensuring a harassment-free environment and promoting gender equality. ICC of Ilahia Law College is constituted on 25/11/2024 with the following committee members.
