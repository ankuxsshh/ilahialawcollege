Anti-Ragging Committee

The Anti-Ragging Committee at Ilahia Law College is a statutory body dedicated to maintaining a safe and inclusive campus environment, free from any form of ragging. The committee actively monitors student interactions, implements preventive measures, and conducts awareness programs to promote mutual respect among students.

Ragging is not just a disciplinary violation; it is a cognizable offense under Indian law. The Kerala Prohibition of Ragging Act, 1998 strictly criminalizes all forms of ragging, with severe penalties, including imprisonment for up to two years and fines of up to ten thousand rupees. Additionally, students found guilty of ragging face immediate dismissal and are permanently disqualified from admission to any educational institution in the state.

The University Grants Commission (UGC) Regulations, 2009, further enforce strict anti-ragging measures across higher educational institutions in India. These regulations mandate severe disciplinary actions such as suspension, expulsion, and rustication for those found guilty of ragging. Ilahia Law College maintains a zero-tolerance policy towards ragging, ensuring that our campus remains a safe space for learning and personal growth.

Women’s Grievance Cell

The Women’s Grievance Cell at Ilahia Law College is committed to ensuring a respectful, harassment-free, and secure environment for female students and staff. This statutory body is responsible for preventing and addressing incidents of sexual harassment, gender discrimination, and other grievances related to women’s safety.

Beyond grievance redressal, the cell actively organizes awareness programs on women’s rights, gender sensitivity, and legal protections available to women. It ensures that all complaints are handled with confidentiality, empathy, and impartiality. Upon receiving a complaint, the cell promptly initiates inquiries, conducts thorough investigations, and recommends necessary actions to the administration. By implementing preventive strategies and promoting a culture of respect and equality, the Women’s Grievance Cell plays a crucial role in upholding the dignity and security of all women on campus.

Students’ Grievance Cell

The Students’ Grievance Cell at Ilahia Law College serves as a platform for students to voice their concerns and seek redressal in a fair and timely manner. Our objective is to foster a positive and harmonious academic environment where students feel heard and supported.

The Cell addresses grievances related to :

  • Academic Concerns – Issues regarding course content, teaching methods, and evaluation processes.
  • Financial Matters – Concerns about tuition fees, scholarships, and other financial support.
  • Campus Facilities – Complaints regarding sanitation, canteen services, transportation, or any other infrastructure-related issues.

By ensuring open communication, maintaining mutual respect, and discouraging actions that cause discord, the Students’ Grievance Cell works proactively to resolve issues in accordance with institutional policies. The cell regularly updates the administration on resolved cases and highlights matters that require higher-level intervention.

At Ilahia Law College, we believe that an inclusive and responsive grievance redressal system is essential to maintaining a supportive and student-friendly educational environment
