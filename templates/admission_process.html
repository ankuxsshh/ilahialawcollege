Admission Procedure for Ilahia Law College

Welcome to Ilahia Law College! Here is the simplified admission process for our 3-year Unitary LLB and 5-year Integrated LLB programs.

1. Check Eligibility

5-year Integrated LLB (BA LLB / BBA LLB / BSc LLB) :

Must have completed 10+2 (or equivalent) from a recognized board with a minimum of [X%] marks.

Age limit may apply (age relaxation for reserved categories).

3-year Unitary LLB :

Must have completed a Bachelor's degree (in any stream) from a recognized university with a minimum of [X%] marks.

Age limit may apply (age relaxation for reserved categories).

2. Entrance Exam (if applicable)

Admission to Ilahia Law College is primarily merit-based.

Candidates may need to appear for national-level entrance exams such as CLAT or AILET.

The college may also conduct its own entrance test for specific programs.

Final selection is based on exam scores, eligibility criteria, and counseling performance.

3. How to Apply

Obtain the application form from the Ilahia Law College Admission Office.

Fill out the form and submit it along with the required documents (mark sheets, ID proof, etc.) to the admission office.

Pay the application fee via [mention payment methods, e.g., cash, demand draft, or bank transfer].

4. Merit List & Counseling

After the entrance exam, a merit list will be published on the Ilahia Law College website.

Shortlisted candidates will be invited for counseling to select their course and confirm admission.

5. Document Verification

During the counseling session, candidates must provide original documents for verification (mark sheets, degree certificates, ID proof, etc.).

6. Fee Payment

Pay the admission fee to confirm your seat in the program after document verification.
