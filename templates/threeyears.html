About Course

This program leads to a Bachelor’s Degree in Law (LL.B.), structured in accordance with the Bar Council of India (BCI) regulations, as empowered by the Advocates Act, 1961. Upon successful completion, the degree qualifies graduates for enrollment as an Advocate under the Advocates Act, 1961. The course requires a minimum of three academic years of regular study. Additionally, the final six months of the last academic year include mandatory practical training, ensuring students gain hands-on legal experience.

Eligibility & Admission Criteria


  • (i) Nativity Requirements:

    • The applicant must be an Indian citizen.
    • However, only candidates of Kerala origin are eligible for reservation benefits and fee concessions.

    To verify Indian citizenship, applicants must upload one of the following documents along with their application:

    • A true copy of the birth certificate, issued by a competent birth registration authority (Panchayat/Municipality/Corporation), showing the place of birth in any State or Union Territory of India.
    • A relevant page of the Secondary School Leaving Certificate (SSLC), indicating the place of birth in any State or Union Territory of India.
    • A valid passport issued by the Government of India.
    • A certificate from a Village Officer or competent authority, certifying that the applicant is a native of any State or Union Territory of India (in the prescribed format).

    To establish Kerala origin, candidates must submit one of the following documents:

    • The relevant page of the SSLC certificate, indicating Kerala as the place of birth.
    • A birth certificate, issued by a competent birth registration authority, showing Kerala as the place of birth.
    • A passport of the candidate or either parent, issued by the Government of India, specifying the place of birth as Kerala. If the parent's passport is uploaded, a corroborative certificate proving the parent-child relationship must also be submitted.
    • The relevant page of the SSLC certificate of either parent, showing Kerala as the place of birth. If the parent’s document is used, a relationship certificate must also be uploaded.
    • A certificate from the Village Officer/ Tahsildar, confirming that the applicant or their mother/father was born in Kerala (in the prescribed format).
  • (ii) Academic Qualifications:

    • The applicant must hold a Bachelor’s Degree in any faculty from a university in Kerala or from any other university recognized as equivalent by a university in Kerala.
    • Candidates must have secured a minimum aggregate of 45% across all parts of their Bachelor’s Degree Examination to qualify for admission to the three-year LL.B. course.
    • Candidates from Socially and Educationally Backward Classes (SEBC) require a minimum of 42% marks, while those from Scheduled Castes (SC) or Scheduled Tribes (ST) need only 40% marks.
    • Rounding off of percentages to the nearest higher integer is not permitted.
    • Applicants who obtained their first-degree certificate through distance education or correspondence courses are eligible for admission. However, those who completed their graduation/post-graduation through an open university system without a prior basic qualification are not eligible.
    • Candidates appearing for or awaiting results of their qualifying examination (regular examination of the 2021-22 academic year) may apply for the Entrance Examination. However, admission will be granted only upon submission of their Provisional/Degree Certificate along with mark lists of all parts of the qualifying examination at the time of admission.
  • (iii) Age Criteria:

    • There is no lower age limit for admission to the three-year LL.B. course.
    • The upper age limit for admission is subject to the decision of the Hon’ble Supreme Court of India.
    • If the age restriction under Clause 28 of the Legal Education Rules, 2008, as prescribed by the Bar Council of India (BCI), is reinstated, only candidates within the stipulated age limit will be permitted to appear for the Entrance Examination.
