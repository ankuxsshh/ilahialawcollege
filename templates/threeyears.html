About Course

The 3-year LL.B. Degree Program is structured in accordance with the Bar Council of India (BCI) regulations under the Advocates Act, 1961. It qualifies graduates for enrollment as Advocates upon successful completion. This course requires a minimum of three academic years of regular study, with the final six months dedicated to mandatory practical training for hands-on legal experience.

Eligibility & Admission Criteria


  • (i) Nativity Requirements:

    • The applicant must be an Indian citizen.
    • Only candidates of Kerala origin are eligible for reservation benefits and fee concessions.

    To verify Indian citizenship, upload any one of the following:

    • Birth certificate issued by competent authority (Panchayat/Municipality/Corporation) showing place of birth in India.
    • Relevant SSLC certificate page showing place of birth in India.
    • Valid Indian passport.
    • Certificate from Village Officer/competent authority verifying Indian nativity (prescribed format).

    To establish Kerala origin, submit any one of the following:

    • SSLC certificate indicating Kerala as place of birth.
    • Birth certificate showing Kerala as place of birth.
    • Passport of candidate or parent showing Kerala as place of birth.
      (Include relationship certificate if parent’s passport is submitted.)
    • SSLC certificate of candidate or parent showing Kerala as place of birth.
      (Include relationship certificate if parent’s certificate is submitted.)
    • Village Officer/Tahsildar certificate confirming birth in Kerala (prescribed format).
  • (ii) Academic Qualifications:

    • Must hold a Bachelor’s Degree from a recognized university in Kerala or elsewhere as recognized by Kerala Universities.
    • Minimum 45% aggregate marks for General category, 42% for SEBC, and 40% for SC/ST candidates.
    • Rounding off marks to the next integer is not permitted.
    • Eligible if the degree was earned via distance or correspondence mode.
    • Not eligible if the degree was earned from an open university system without basic qualifications.
    • Candidates appearing for final exams may apply but must submit proof of qualification at admission time.
  • (iii) Age Criteria:

    • No lower age limit.
    • Upper age limit subject to Supreme Court decision.
    • If BCI's Clause 28 age limit is reinstated, only eligible candidates can appear for the Entrance Exam.

Fees and Funding

Scholarships and financial aid are available for meritorious students. The course fee covers tuition, library access, and internship programs.
